Message

For Jayda

To my Iris, Jayda. I’m really sorry for how much I hurt you, and I didn’t mean to do it on purpose by leaving you that day; I really didn’t plan on leaving you, but my mind is telling me that I have to let you go because if I continue pursuing you, you'll be hurt even more. Hindi ko ginustong iwanan ka. And I know you were hurt by what I did, and deep inside, I’m still in pain when you're in pain too. And I know that the past is past, but it always comes to my mind and my heart. I still remember the day you treated me like your boyfriend, kakampi, or husband kahit manliligaw mo palang ako; how you gave me some advice; how you comforted me when I had a big problem especially nung na-hospital si Mama; and how much you loved me more than yourself. I feel you too. I know that we’re done na, but I want to see you, comfort you, hug you, and talk to you in person—not to fix things, but for closure.

I loved you, Jayda. I really did. Not in the loud, reckless way, but in the way a man stays when it would’ve been easier to leave. I fought for us quietly, consistently, with patience I didn’t even know I had. I bent parts of myself just to make space for you, and I never once thought of it as a loss back then. I thought love was supposed to hurt a little, supposed to ask you to endure, but loving you didn’t save us; effort didn’t turn into a miracle. All that wanting, all that choosing, still wasn’t enough to change the ending, and that’s the part I keep sitting with—the idea that you can do everything right and still lose someone. No grand betrayal, just two people wanting different things at different depths. I replay everything in my head, not because I think I can fix it, but because part of me refuses to let it be small. What we had mattered to me. You mattered to me. I hate how easy it looks from the outside, like it was just another story that ended. It wasn’t. It lived in me. It shaped the way I speak, the way I wait, the way I love now. I don’t blame myself the way I used to. I showed up. I stayed honest. I loved you in the only way I knew how—fully, even when it scared me. If that wasn’t enough, then maybe it was never about my lack, but about timing, about alignment, about things no amount of fighting could fix. I’ll miss you without chasing you. I’ll remember you without reopening wounds. And one day, the yearning will soften into something quieter—not gone, just gentler. Until then, I’ll let myself feel it because loving you was real, and losing you doesn’t erase that.

Happy birthday! I don't really know where we stand now, but I do know one thing: you were once someone very important in my life, and that feeling didn't disappear just like that. I hope today you feel appreciated, loved, and at peace, even if that peace doesn't come from me anymore.

Thank you for the memories, the lessons, and even the pain, because all of it changed me into who I am. I wish you growth, happiness, and a life that treats you kindly. And somewhere in my heart, I still hope you're okay. Always.

- Jas
